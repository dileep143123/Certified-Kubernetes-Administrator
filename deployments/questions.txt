CKA: DEPLOYMENTS — QUESTIONS & ANSWERS (Detailed Understanding)
==============================================================

FOUNDATION
----------

1) Q: What is a Deployment?
   A: A Kubernetes controller that manages stateless apps by maintaining replicas,
      performing rolling updates, and supporting rollbacks.

2) Q: What is the core object chain for Deployments?
   A: Deployment -> ReplicaSet -> Pods

3) Q: Does a Deployment create pods directly?
   A: No. It creates/updates ReplicaSets. ReplicaSets create pods.

4) Q: Why use Deployment instead of ReplicaSet?
   A: Deployment provides rolling updates, rollbacks, and rollout history. RS only maintains
      replica count.

UNDER THE HOOD (ROLLING UPDATE)
-------------------------------

5) Q: What happens when you update the container image in a Deployment?
   A:
   - Deployment creates a new ReplicaSet for the new Pod template.
   - New RS scales up while old RS scales down gradually (RollingUpdate).

6) Q: Why do you see multiple ReplicaSets after updating a Deployment?
   A: Each change in Pod template creates a new ReplicaSet (new revision). Old RS is kept
      for rollback history.

7) Q: What is the purpose of revisionHistoryLimit?
   A: It controls how many old ReplicaSets are retained for rollbacks.

8) Q: What does maxSurge mean?
   A: How many extra pods can be created above desired replicas during a rolling update.

9) Q: What does maxUnavailable mean?
   A: How many pods are allowed to be unavailable during a rolling update.

10) Q: When would you use strategy: Recreate?
    A: When you cannot run old and new versions at the same time (downtime is acceptable).

ROLLBACK / HISTORY (CKA MUST KNOW)
---------------------------------

11) Q: How do you watch rollout progress?
    A: kubectl rollout status deploy/<name>

12) Q: How do you view rollout history?
    A: kubectl rollout history deploy/<name>

13) Q: How do you rollback a deployment?
    A: kubectl rollout undo deploy/<name>

14) Q: How do you rollback to a specific revision?
    A: kubectl rollout undo deploy/<name> --to-revision=<N>

DEBUGGING (CKA STYLE)
---------------------

15) Q: Rollout is stuck. What is the first command you run?
    A: kubectl rollout status deploy/<name> and kubectl describe deploy/<name> (Events)

16) Q: New pods are Running but the rollout still doesn’t complete. Why?
    A: Pods might not be Ready (readinessProbe failing). Deployment waits for readiness.

17) Q: What are the best places to find the real root cause of a failed rollout?
    A:
    - kubectl describe deploy/<name> (Events)
    - kubectl describe rs <new-rs> (Events)
    - kubectl describe pod <pod> (Events)
    - kubectl logs <pod> / --previous

18) Q: Deployment desired replicas is 3 but available replicas is 0. What could be wrong?
    A:
    - ImagePullBackOff / ErrImagePull
    - FailedScheduling
    - CrashLoopBackOff
    - readinessProbe failing
    Check Events + logs.

19) Q: How do you confirm which ReplicaSet is the current one?
    A: kubectl get rs
       The newest RS usually has desired/current replicas > 0.
       Also check pod-template-hash label; describe deployment shows current/old RS.

20) Q: Why might Service traffic fail after a Deployment update?
    A:
    - Service endpoints include only Ready pods.
    - If new pods are not Ready, endpoints may be empty or still point to old pods.
    Check: kubectl get endpoints <svc>

PRACTICAL COMMANDS
------------------

21) Q: How do you create, update image, and rollback (3 commands)?
    A:
    - kubectl create deploy web --image=nginx:1.25-alpine
    - kubectl set image deploy/web web=nginx:1.26-alpine
    - kubectl rollout undo deploy/web

22) Q: How do you scale a deployment?
    A: kubectl scale deploy/<name> --replicas=<N>

EXAM SUMMARY
------------

23) Q: In one sentence, what is a Deployment’s job?
    A: Maintain desired replicas and manage safe updates/rollbacks by controlling ReplicaSets.

24) Q: In one sentence, what is the biggest difference between Deployment and ReplicaSet?
    A: ReplicaSet keeps N pods running; Deployment manages ReplicaSets to handle versioned rollouts and rollbacks.
